Lets know about Alzheimer

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder characterized by the gradual decline of cognitive functions, impacting memory, thinking, and behavior. It is the most common cause of dementia among older adults. In the context of medical imaging, particularly Flair MRI (Fluid-Attenuated Inversion Recovery) is utilized to visualize brain structures and detect abnormalities. Flair MRI is sensitive to fluid changes and is effective in highlighting lesions, making it valuable for examining brain tissues. In Alzheimer's research, Flair MRI plays a crucial role in identifying structural alterations and abnormalities associated with the disease, aiding in early diagnosis and understanding the progression of this challenging neurological condition.

Four Types of Alzheimer

Mild Cognitive Impairment

Mild Cognitive Impairment

Mild cognitive impairment (MCI) is a condition that represents a subtle but noticeable decline in cognitive abilities, beyond what is considered normal for a person's age.

Very Mild Cognitive Impairment

Very Mild Cognitive Impairment

Very mild cognitive impairment (VMCI) is a subtle decline in cognitive abilities that is more noticeable than typical age-related changes but does not significantly impact daily functioning.

Moderate Cognitive Impairment

Moderate Cognitive Impairment

Moderate cognitive impairment represents a more noticeable decline in cognitive functions compared to mild stages but falls short of severe impairment.

Non-Cognitive Impairment

Non-Cognitive Impairment

Non-cognitive impairment refers to a condition where there is no significant decline in cognitive functions.
